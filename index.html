Welcome to WELL.NESS, a community mapping project
centered on health narratives from the Inglewood Oil Field.

This project seeks to serve the communities and individuals living near the Inglewood Oil Field in Los Angeles. Neighborhoods surrounding the Inglewood Oil Field are at risk of developing negative health effects due to the toxins released from both active and inactive (but unsealed) neighborhood oil wells. These neighborhoods are often overrepresented by people of color, especially Black and Latinx populations.

To highlight the voices of community members and their experiences living in this region, we have designed a short survey that any community member can take in English or Spanish. After taking the survey, you will see your response anonymously recorded on our site and mapped according to your zip code.

When interacting on the site, the "Stories" tab will display survey responses, while the "Summary" tab will display the race/ethnicity breakdown of a specific zip code based on survey responses, as well as other key information. The intensity of the color red depicts the number of responses in each zip code.

Even if you choose not to contribute your story at this time or are simply interested in exploring the site, we hope this website acts as a database of community knowledge and a source of empowerment in the fight for health justice.
